The History of Burgers: From Hamburg to Ghana

EB
Edwin Boadi
January 15, 2026 5 min read

The humble hamburger has become one of the most iconic foods in the world, and its journey from the streets of Hamburg, Germany to the bustling markets of Accra, Ghana is a fascinating tale of culinary evolution and cultural adaptation.

The Origins: Hamburg, Germany

The story begins in the 19th century in the German city of Hamburg. German immigrants brought with them a dish called "Hamburg steak" – a minced beef patty seasoned with onions and spices. When these immigrants arrived in America, they brought this culinary tradition with them.

By the late 1800s, American cooks had begun placing the Hamburg steak between two slices of bread for easier eating on the go. The exact inventor is disputed, but what's certain is that by 1904, the hamburger was already a popular item at the St. Louis World's Fair.

The Global Journey

Throughout the 20th century, the hamburger spread across the globe, adapted by each culture it touched:

  • In Japan, it became the "hambāgu," often served with rice
  • In Australia, they added beetroot and fried eggs
  • In Mexico, jalapeños and salsa became standard toppings
  • And in Ghana, we've added our own unique twist...

The Burger Comes to Ghana

Fast food culture began emerging in Ghana in the 1990s, but it was the 2000s that saw the real burger revolution. International chains introduced Ghanaians to the concept, but it was local entrepreneurs who made it their own.

At SWIFT CHOW, we honor this tradition while embracing our Ghanaian heritage. Our Spicy Jerk Burger, for instance, combines the classic American format with Caribbean and West African spices that our customers love. Our Meat Pies, while not burgers, represent another form of this cultural fusion – taking a British concept and making it unmistakably Ghanaian.

The Future of Burgers in Ghana

As we look ahead, we see exciting possibilities. More local ingredients, sustainable practices, and innovative flavor combinations. The burger's journey isn't over – it's simply entering its next chapter, and we're proud to be writing it here in Ghana.

Next time you bite into one of our burgers, remember: you're not just eating a sandwich. You're partaking in a culinary tradition that spans continents, centuries, and cultures. That's something worth savoring.
